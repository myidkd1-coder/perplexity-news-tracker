## Summary
New animal research shows that transfusing young blood into mice can slow or reduce Alzheimer’s-like brain changes, while older blood accelerates memory decline and pathology. The work suggests blood-borne factors play a key role in neurodegeneration and could point to novel therapeutic strategies, though translation to humans will require extensive further study.
