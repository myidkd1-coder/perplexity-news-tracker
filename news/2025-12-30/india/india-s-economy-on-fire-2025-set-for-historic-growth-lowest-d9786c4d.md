India Poised for Stellar Economic Year in 2025

The Indian government has released its year-end economic review, projecting 2025 to be a landmark year for the nation's economic performance. The outlook is characterized by strong growth across key sectors, persistently low inflation, expanding exports, and an improving labor market.

Robust Growth Metrics

Real Gross Domestic Product (GDP) surged by 8.2 percent in the second quarter of the financial year 2025-26, marking a six-quarter high. This resilience in domestic demand persists despite a challenging global trade environment. The growth in real Gross Value Added (GVA), a measure of domestic production, stood at 8.1 percent for the same period. This broad-based expansion is largely fueled by significant buoyancy in both the industrial and services sectors, signaling a widespread upturn in economic activity.

Benign Inflation and Monetary Support

Inflationary pressures have remained notably subdued throughout the year. The Consumer Price Index (CPI) inflation rate displayed a consistent downward trend, softening from 4.26 percent in January 2025 to an exceptional 0.71 percent by November 2025. This price stability offers the Reserve Bank of India (RBI) ample policy space to maintain its supportive monetary stance, aiding economic expansion.

The Wholesale Price Index (WPI) inflation also moderated, further reinforcing the overall price stability across the economy.

Improving Employment Landscape

Latest labor force estimates reveal a significant improvement in job market conditions. In November 2025, the national unemployment rate declined to 4.7 percent, down from 5.2 percent in October. This represents the lowest recorded unemployment rate since April 2025, with improvements seen across both urban and rural segments.

Encouraging trends were also observed in the overall labor force participation and worker participation rates.

Export Strength and External Resilience

India's export performance demonstrated notable strength over 2025. Merchandise exports reached $38.13 billion in November, up from $36.43 billion at the start of the year. Services exports, a critical component of the external sector, also posted solid growth, reflecting India's increasing integration into global value chains.

External sector resilience is further underscored by robust foreign exchange reserves and an improved current account dynamic, supported by strong remittances and services export receipts.

A "Goldilocks Moment"

The government attributes this favorable economic situation to a combination of strong domestic demand, ongoing structural reforms, supportive monetary policies, and stable prices. This unique blend has led to what is described as a "Goldilocks moment" for the Indian economy, characterized by high growth and low inflation.

With a GDP now exceeding $4.18 trillion, India has cemented its position as the world's fourth-largest economy. Projections from various institutions indicate sustained expansion, highlighting India's potential for robust growth through 2026 and beyond. The government remains committed to implementing policies and structural reforms to maintain economic momentum and foster inclusive growth.
