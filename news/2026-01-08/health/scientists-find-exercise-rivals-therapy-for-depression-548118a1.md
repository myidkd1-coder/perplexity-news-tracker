## Summary
A large review of clinical studies finds that regular exercise can reduce symptoms of depression about as effectively as structured psychological therapy. The analysis suggests exercise offers comparable benefits to antidepressant medications, though the evidence base is less extensive. Authors highlight that even light activity may help and call for integrating exercise into standard depression care.
