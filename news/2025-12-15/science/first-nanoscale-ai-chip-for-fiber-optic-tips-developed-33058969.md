10 The First Nanoscale AI Chip for Fiber Optics

In a critical step toward ultra-efficient quantum communication and medical imaging, researchers successfully developed an AI chip smaller than a grain of salt that can be mounted directly on the tip of an optical fiber. This chip uses a “diffractive neural network” to process images by harnessing the natural diffraction of light itself, eliminating the need for bulky electronic components or external processors.

This breakthrough tackles two major constraints in miniaturized technology: power consumption and speed. By processing information at the speed of light using passive optical layers, the chip drastically reduces energy use and heat generation. Diffractive neural networks require no electricity to compute—they’re essentially stacks of tiny, precisely patterned surfaces that perform calculations as light passes through.

The immediate impact is in two key areas. First, it enables real-time, high-resolution medical imaging inside the human body via tiny fiber-optic endoscopes. Second, it lays the foundation for securing low-energy quantum communication networks by enabling fast, local processing of delicate quantum optical signals.[1]
